Stories

Though Canberra is arguably a quite city known mainly for its political history, the city has also home to some scary haunted places. Out of all, four are the ones that might be regarded as the most spiritually active: Blundells cottage, The National Museum, Hotel Kurrajong and The National Film Archive.

Blundells Cottage

Built in the 1860, the cottage was surronded by farming land and crops. In 1874 the Blundells family moved in. The legend says that George and Flora Blundells' daughter sadly passed away when she was only a teenager. Cause of her death? Burnt alive. It is said that the poor girl was ironing when eventually an unescapable fire took place taking the young girl's life. To these days, visitors witness paranormal activities happening in that place. The most recurrent paranormal phenomenon is a smell of burning flashes. Perhaps, an evidence of the brutal and sudden death that still keeps the soul of the poor girl on this world.

The National Museum

Probably, the least place that comes to mind when thinking about spooky places in Canberra. However, it is said that in one of the rooms of the building many visitors have seen an old woman in a bathtub. There's only a problem though, neither the woman nor the bathtub is phisically there. The National Museum stands where there previously was the Royal Canberra Hospital and the woman might still have not find her way out of a building that no longer exhist. Will she be stuck in the Museum till the end of times?

Hotel Kurrajong

Hotel Kurrajong is one of the buildings where the history of the young capital of Australia has been made. Together with what is now known as the Hyatt Hotel Canberra, it used to be a place where politicians stayed while on duty. However, although almost a century has passed, someone never left. He's name is Ben Chiffley former prime minister of Australia. He suffered a fatal heart attack while in his room and people say that a part of him is still there. Many have witnessed what is considered to be his ghost. What does it look like? When appearing, it usally manifests itself as a middle-age man wearing a grey suit who from the window of his room look toward the old Parliament House.

National Film Archive

Can horror movies function as a bridge to allow for entities in the afterlife to come in our world? Well, the reality of this place is a bit darker. Before being converted to a film archive, this building was home to the Australian Institue of Anatomy. The original idea was to create a space dedicated to science. Thus, exposed to the public there weren't only dead animal bodies but also human ones. Little did they know that messing with the dead is not a good idea. Both visitors and staff members have experienced paranormal activities. Such activities range from objects falling from anti-gravity shelves to creepier ones like seeing children faces appearing from behind grates.
